The Craft

Materials
Matter.

We don't cut corners on material science. Every Liquisity case goes through a rigorous multi-stage process before it ever reaches your hands.

How it's made

The Chrome
Process.

Step 01

Alloy Casting

We start with a zinc-aluminium alloy, chosen for its lightweight properties and exceptional receptivity to electroplating. Molten alloy is injected at precision pressure into CNC-milled tooling — producing near-zero flash and perfectly consistent wall thickness throughout.

Step 02

CNC Finishing

Post-cast, each shell passes through a 5-axis CNC machine that cuts the precise profile — including the 0.1mm lip around the hinge that ensures the lid engages cleanly, and the port clearances that allow full USB-C access without modification.

Step 03

Electroplating

The alloy surface is prepared through alkaline degreasing, acid activation, and copper pre-plate before the chrome layer is deposited in a controlled bath — ensuring uniform adhesion and that characteristic mirror depth. The layer thickness is 0.3–0.5μm.

Step 04

Polish & QC

Six manual polishing stages with progressively finer compounds bring the surface to a clarity rating of Ra <0.05μm — smoother than optical glass. Every unit is then hand-inspected under 5000K lighting and measured with a surface profilometer before packing.

Care Steps

Simple routine,
lasting brilliance.

💧

Daily Wipe

Use a soft, lint-free microfibre cloth for a daily wipe. This removes fingerprint oils before they can oxidise on the surface. Takes 10 seconds. No liquid required daily — dry cloth only.

✨

Weekly Polish

Once a week, apply one drop of isopropyl alcohol (70%) to your cloth and wipe in circular motions. This removes built-up residue and refreshes the mirror surface. Never use abrasive cloths.

🚫

What to Avoid

Never use bleach-based cleaners, acetone, or scouring pads. These will strip the chrome layer permanently. Avoid prolonged contact with salt water — rinse immediately if exposed to the sea.

📦

Storage

When not in use, store in the included matte black pouch. This prevents micro-scratches from keys and coins in your bag. The pouch is made from 300gsm merino wool felt — sourced in New Zealand.

🔧

Deep Restoration

For deeper scratches or haze, Liquisity offers a Re-Polish service. Send your case to our workshop and we'll return it to factory condition within 5 business days for just €24 — including return shipping.

☀️

Heat & UV

The chrome plating is UV-stable — no yellowing or fading in sunlight. Avoid leaving in enclosed hot spaces above 60°C (a parked car in summer) for extended periods, as this may affect the hinge magnet strength over time.

Liquisity was founded on a simple frustration: every AirPods case on the market felt like an afterthought. Plastic, silicone, leather — nothing matched the precision of the device inside it. We decided to change that.

We didn't start with
a product.
We started with a standard.

The Liquisity founding team spent two years in workshops — testing alloy compositions, plating chemistries, and hinge mechanisms — before releasing a single unit to the public. We refused to ship anything that didn't feel exceptional in hand.

Drop 000 — the Void Black — sold out in under 6 minutes. We knew we were onto something. Since then, every drop has sold out within the hour of going live.

Our workshop is in Tallinn, Estonia — a city with a quiet tradition of precision manufacturing and an obsession with getting things right. We work with a team of 12 people, each one responsible for a specific stage of the process.

We are not a drop-shipping brand. We are not a print-on-demand operation. Every Liquisity case is designed in-house, tooled in-house, and quality-controlled in-house. We know who made your case. That matters to us.

Our ambition is simple: to make the best AirPods case on earth. Not the cheapest. Not the most colourful. The best — the one that, five years from now, still looks as good as the day you unboxed it.
